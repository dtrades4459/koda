About · Founder

The journal that holds you to your rules.

Built by one trader, for traders who want to stop sabotaging themselves.

The problem

Most trading journals are graveyards. You log a trade after the damage is done, scroll your stats once a week, and tell yourself "next time I'll cut earlier" — then do the exact same thing on Monday. The journal isn't the intervention; you are. And you're tilting.

I built Kōda because the spreadsheets I'd been using for six years couldn't see me about to break my own rules. By the time the data was logged, it was already a loss. I wanted something that would step in before the over-trade, not catalogue it after.

The wedge

Kōda watches your live trading session for five behavioural signals: consecutive losses, percent of daily loss-limit hit, daily trade cap reached, log-button taps within 10 minutes of a loss, and tilt-emotion tags on your last trade. When one critical signal fires (or two non-critical ones combine), the Log Trade form doesn't open — an intervention sheet does. You can override it. Or you can take the cooldown. Most days, that's enough.

No machine learning. No broker API guess. Just your own logged activity, surfaced one beat ahead of where you usually act.

The journal isn't the intervention; you are. And you're tilting.

Who's behind it

Kōda is built by Dylon Nyland, a futures trader (NQ / ES, prop-funded) based in the UK. I write the code, design the interfaces, and use the product daily as my own primary journal. There's no team behind a curtain — when you email support, you're emailing me.

Stack: TypeScript, React 19, Supabase, Vercel. Privacy-first: your trades, your rules, your data, your Supabase row.

Where it's going

Closed beta now. Public launch summer 2026. The roadmap focuses on three things: making the intervention smarter without making it a black box, growing the Trading Circles social layer for accountability, and building out the prop-firm eval tracker into a proper challenge co-pilot.

If you trade futures, log every fill, and want a journal that talks back when you're about to make the same mistake — try it. Get on the waitlist.
